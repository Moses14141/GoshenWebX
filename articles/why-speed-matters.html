Why Speed Matters: Core Web Vitals Explained

The three numbers Google uses to decide if your website deserves to rank — and how to win them

In 2026, website speed is no longer just about user experience — it is a direct ranking signal that decides whether your small business appears on page 1 or page 10 of Google.

What Are Core Web Vitals?

Core Web Vitals are three specific measurements Google uses to evaluate the real-world experience of your website: Largest Contentful Paint (LCP), Interaction to Next Paint (INP), and Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS). They focus on loading speed, responsiveness, and visual stability.

1. Largest Contentful Paint (LCP) – How Fast Your Page Feels Loaded

LCP measures how long it takes for the largest piece of content (usually the hero image or main headline) to appear on screen. Google wants this under 2.5 seconds. Anything slower and visitors start leaving — and Google starts lowering your rankings.

2. Interaction to Next Paint (INP) – How Responsive Your Site Feels

INP replaces the old FID metric and measures how quickly your site responds when someone clicks, taps, or types. A good score is under 200 milliseconds. Slow response times make your site feel broken — even if it eventually loads.

3. Cumulative Layout Shift (CLS) – No More Jumping Content

CLS measures how much your page layout unexpectedly shifts while loading. A score under 0.1 is excellent. High CLS frustrates users (think buttons moving just as you click them) and hurts both conversions and rankings.

Core Web Vitals at a Glance

Largest Contentful Paint

Goal: Under 2.5 seconds

Interaction to Next Paint

Goal: Under 200 ms

Cumulative Layout Shift

Goal: Under 0.1

Why Most AI-Built Websites Fail These Metrics

AI website builders often produce heavy, unoptimised code that fails Core Web Vitals. Professional development ensures every image is properly sized, every script is deferred, and every layout element is stable — delivering scores that actually rank.
